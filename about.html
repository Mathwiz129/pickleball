About Chattpickleball

About

Chattpickleball is a really barebones app built for a small group of Chattanooga-area players who just want to track games. It’s not meant for broad public use (at least not yet), but if you’ve stumbled across it you can definitely use it-just be careful.

Technically, data is public. But there’s no search or indexing, and unless you’re poking around in developer tools, most info is hard to get to. It’s not secure in a traditional sense, but for our tiny use case, it's fine for now.

If this ever does expand-adding logins, match verification, privacy layers-I’m open to it. But right now it’s a side project designed for a specific group. Feedback always welcome.

How Rankings Are Calculated

Each player’s rating is calculated on a scale from 1.00 to 10.00, based on:

  • Win ratio (wins ÷ matches played)
  • Average scoring margin (points scored minus points allowed per match)
  • Match volume (slight penalty applied if fewer than 3 total matches)

The formula used to calculate each player’s rating is:

rating = 5 + ((winRatio - 0.5) × 10) + (avgMargin ÷ 5) - penalty

Where:

  • winRatio = wins / total matches
  • avgMargin = (total points scored - total points allowed) / total matches
  • penalty = 1.0 if matches played < 3, otherwise 0

Ratings are clamped between 1.00 and 10.00 to keep things fair and intuitive. Players start near 5.00 and rise as they win with consistency and strong margins.

Example

If a player has played 8 matches with 6 wins, scored 88 points and allowed 64: 

winRatio = 6 / 8 = 0.75
avgMargin = (88 - 64) / 8 = 3
rating = 5 + ((0.75 - 0.5) × 10) + (3 ÷ 5) = 8.10

If that same player had only played 2 matches, a 1.0 penalty would lower their rating to 7.10.
